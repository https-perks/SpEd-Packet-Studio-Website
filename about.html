Why I made this

I was spending too much time creating the tools my staff needed before they could even use them.

When it came time to prepare accommodations pages, data sheets, and IEP information for teachers, paraprofessionals, and other staff, I had to put real time and effort into creating something clear and practical for them. The information already existed, but turning it into resources people could actually use became a separate project of its own.

One program might work for accommodations sheets. Another might be better for data collection. Then I would need a different document or system to share the most important IEP information with teachers. Each piece had to be created, formatted, updated, and kept consistent with everything else.

I wanted a one-and-done workflow: one place where I could keep the original student information together, decide exactly what belonged in each packet, and create every page the team needed without rebuilding the same work across multiple programs.

Just as importantly, I wanted the final packet to feel cohesive and well built. Accommodations, goals, service information, data sheets, and observation tools should look like parts of the same resource—not a collection of unrelated forms assembled at the last minute.

I also wanted more flexibility than the paper and spreadsheet templates commonly available to teachers. Those templates can be helpful, but they often make the educator adapt to the form. SpEd Packet Studio lets educators customize the packet instead: choose the relevant sections, shape the data tools, create versions for different audiences, and keep the source information connected in one place.

That is why I created SpEd Packet Studio: to spend less time assembling documents and more time giving teachers, paraprofessionals, and staff clear information they can confidently use to support students.

Ryan Perkins Special educator and creator of SpEd Packet Studio
What guides the app

Built around a few
non-negotiable ideas.

01

One source of truth.

Keep the original student information together instead of recreating and maintaining it across disconnected files.

02

Customization that serves the team.

Educators should be able to choose the pages, details, data tools, and packet versions their staff actually need.

03

Cohesion builds clarity.

A thoughtfully designed packet is easier to scan, easier to understand, and easier for a team to use consistently.

04

Time belongs with students.

Every repetitive formatting task removed creates more room for collaboration, instruction, and student support.
